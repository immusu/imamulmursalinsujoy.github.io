Heart Disease Prediction System

The Heart Disease Prediction System is an innovative prototype designed to leverage Artificial Intelligence for predicting the likelihood of heart disease in individuals. This system was developed as a comprehensive project, utilizing a historical dataset of heart disease to train and validate various AI models. The core functionality of the system is based on multiple AI algorithms, including K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machine (SVM), and Decision Tree classifiers. Each model was meticulously trained and tested on the dataset to evaluate their predictive accuracy and reliability. The dataset included a range of clinical parameters such as age, gender, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other relevant health metrics. To develop this system, I first preprocessed the data to handle missing values, normalize features, and split the data into training and testing sets. I then implemented the KNN algorithm, which classifies a patient's condition based on the closest data points in the feature space. Next, I used the SVM model, which finds the optimal hyperplane that separates the classes of patients with and without heart disease. Finally, the Decision Tree model was implemented to create a tree-like structure of decisions based on the input features. After training the models, I conducted a comprehensive comparison of their performance using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. This comparative analysis revealed the strengths and weaknesses of each model in predicting heart disease. Additionally, I implemented cross-validation techniques to ensure the robustness and generalizability of the models. The Heart Disease Prediction System provides an interactive interface where users can input their health data and receive an immediate prediction of their heart disease risk. This prototype demonstrates the potential of AI in healthcare, offering a valuable tool for early diagnosis and preventive care.

Fire Detection System

The Fire Detection System is a comprehensive hardware prototype designed to enhance fire safety through early detection and real-time alerts. The system employs a highly sensitive fire sensor, which promptly identifies the presence of fire or smoke. Additionally, the MQ-02 gas sensor is integrated to detect combustible gases, providing an extra layer of hazard detection. Upon detecting a fire or hazardous gas levels, the system leverages the HC-05 Bluetooth module to transmit immediate hazard notifications to connected smartphones. This ensures that users receive timely alerts, allowing for quick response and mitigation of potential fire-related damages. The Bluetooth module's reliable communication capabilities facilitate seamless connectivity between the detection system and the users' mobile devices. The project also involves programming microcontrollers to process sensor data and manage the communication between sensors and smartphones. The system's design emphasizes ease of installation and user-friendliness, making it suitable for various environments such as homes, offices, and industrial settings.

Digital Clock System

The core of the system is the 555 timer IC, configured to generate precise timing pulses. These pulses are fed into the 7493 binary counter IC, which counts the pulses and translates them into a binary format. The binary output from the 7493 IC is then decoded and displayed on the seven-segment displays, representing the hours, minutes, and seconds in a 24-hour format. This ensures accurate and readable time presentation. To enhance functionality, the Digital Clock System includes a stopwatch mode, allowing users to measure elapsed time with precision. By integrating control switches, users can start, stop, and reset the stopwatch as needed. This dual functionality makes the system versatile and practical for various timekeeping needs. The synchronization feature is another highlight of this project. The clock can be synchronized with external clock frequencies, ensuring it remains accurate even when integrated into different systems or environments. This capability is achieved through adjustable frequency dividers and synchronization circuits, demonstrating the adaptability and precision of the design. During the development process, careful attention was paid to the electrical design and circuit layout to ensure stability and accuracy. The project involved soldering components, designing circuit boards, and extensive testing to fine-tune the system's performance.
