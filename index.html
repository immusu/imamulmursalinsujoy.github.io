Research

Monocular Camera Fusion for Enhanced Depth Sensing & Object Detection

Supervisor: Dr. Md. Khalilur Rhaman, Professor, Department of CSE, BRAC University

In this era of technological advancement, autonomous vehicles strive towards revolutionizing the transportation system. A critical challenge in this domain is obstacle depth sensing in different environmental scenarios which is vital for ensuring safety. This research paper proposes an innovative approach MiDYOLO. This evolved approach is capable of addressing these challenges of obstacle depth sensing by incorporating monocular camera fusion with a dynamic algorithm for accurate depth detection and object identification. For detecting obstacles, autonomous vehicles rely on an array of sensors, including a combination of LiDAR, Radar, and cameras each of which has its strengths and limitations. Moreover, traditional modal systems combining LiDAR or Radar with camera vision often have efficiency and cost related drawbacks such as high cost or power demand and spatial resolution constraints.

Furthermore, Bangladesh's road networks and challenging weather conditions makes the process more challenging. In order to optimize obstacle depth sensing based on these diverse scenarios and dynamic environmental conditions, our research proposes a concept with the fusion of monocular cameras with merging algorithms. Building upon the capabilities of YOLOv7 serving the purpose of object identification and Midas for accurate depth detection, MiDYOLO incorporates them in a seamless way to create an approach that delivers enhanced depth perception and object categorization. Alongside offering a promising trajectory for safer and more adaptive transportation in the future, our proposed system MiDYOLO ensures efficiency and accuracy in obstacle depth detection.

Heart Disease Prediction System

The Heart Disease Prediction System is an innovative prototype designed to leverage Artificial Intelligence for predicting the likelihood of heart disease in individuals. This system was developed as a comprehensive project, utilizing a historical dataset of heart disease to train and validate various AI models. The core functionality of the system is based on multiple AI algorithms, including K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machine (SVM), and Decision Tree classifiers. Each model was meticulously trained and tested on the dataset to evaluate their predictive accuracy and reliability. The dataset included a range of clinical parameters such as age, gender, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other relevant health metrics. To develop this system, I first preprocessed the data to handle missing values, normalize features, and split the data into training and testing sets. I then implemented the KNN algorithm, which classifies a patient's condition based on the closest data points in the feature space. Next, I used the SVM model, which finds the optimal hyperplane that separates the classes of patients with and without heart disease. Finally, the Decision Tree model was implemented to create a tree-like structure of decisions based on the input features.

After training the models, I conducted a comprehensive comparison of their performance using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. This comparative analysis revealed the strengths and weaknesses of each model in predicting heart disease. Additionally, I implemented cross-validation techniques to ensure the robustness and generalizability of the models. The Heart Disease Prediction System provides an interactive interface where users can input their health data and receive an immediate prediction of their heart disease risk. This prototype demonstrates the potential of AI in healthcare, offering a valuable tool for early diagnosis and preventive care.

Fire Detection System

The Fire Detection System is a comprehensive hardware prototype designed to enhance fire safety through early detection and real-time alerts. The system employs a highly sensitive fire sensor, which promptly identifies the presence of fire or smoke. Additionally, the MQ-02 gas sensor is integrated to detect combustible gases, providing an extra layer of hazard detection. Upon detecting a fire or hazardous gas levels, the system leverages the HC-05 Bluetooth module to transmit immediate hazard notifications to connected smartphones. This ensures that users receive timely alerts, allowing for quick response and mitigation of potential fire-related damages.

The Bluetooth module's reliable communication capabilities facilitate seamless connectivity between the detection system and the users' mobile devices. The project also involves programming microcontrollers to process sensor data and manage the communication between sensors and smartphones. The system's design emphasizes ease of installation and user-friendliness, making it suitable for various environments such as homes, offices, and industrial settings.

Digital Clock System

The core of the system is the 555 timer IC, configured to generate precise timing pulses. These pulses are fed into the 7493 binary counter IC, which counts the pulses and translates them into a binary format. The binary output from the 7493 IC is then decoded and displayed on the seven-segment displays, representing the hours, minutes, and seconds in a 24-hour format. This ensures accurate and readable time presentation. To enhance functionality, the Digital Clock System includes a stopwatch mode, allowing users to measure elapsed time with precision. By integrating control switches, users can start, stop, and reset the stopwatch as needed. This dual functionality makes the system versatile and practical for various timekeeping needs.

The synchronization feature is another highlight of this project. The clock can be synchronized with external clock frequencies, ensuring it remains accurate even when integrated into different systems or environments. This capability is achieved through adjustable frequency dividers and synchronization circuits, demonstrating the adaptability and precision of the design. During the development process, careful attention was paid to the electrical design and circuit layout to ensure stability and accuracy. The project involved soldering components, designing circuit boards, and extensive testing to fine-tune the system's performance.
